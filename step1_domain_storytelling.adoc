=== Story A — Happy Path

[cols="1,8", options="header"]
|===
| Step | What happened

| 1
| Steel panel #A-4471 arrived on Conveyor 3 at 06:42. Speed 0.4 m/s. Morning shift, normal lighting conditions.

| 2
| The edge vision node EV-07 captured four overlapping frames at 2400 dpi and ran inference in 140 ms.

| 3
| A 12 mm linear scratch on the top-left quadrant was detected. Confidence score 0.91. Defect class: SurfaceScratch.

| 4
| The system looked up the active decision band: confidence above 0.85 maps to Decision = SCRAP. Decision was auto-assigned without human input.

| 5
| The panel diverter triggered at station 4. A red warning light flashed at Petra's HMI workstation along with a thumbnail of the detected area.

| 6
| Petra reviewed the thumbnail and agreed with the decision. She logged no override. Duration from alert to acknowledgement: 28 seconds.

| 7
| InspectionCase #IC-4471 was closed: Decision = SCRAP, Override = none, Operator = Petra K.

| 8
| The closed case record was forwarded to the Case Management system for archival and downstream RCA eligibility check.
|===

=== Story B — Failure Mode / Edge Case

[cols="1,8", options="header"]
|===
| Step | What happened

| 1
| Planned maintenance replaced halogen lighting with LED on Line 3 at 14:00 during shift handover. Nobody notified the ML team.

| 2
| The edge model continued running with calibration trained under halogen spectrum. No mechanism existed to detect an illumination change in the inference pipeline.

| 3
| The model began flagging 38–42% of panels as SurfaceScratch with confidence scores between 0.86 and 0.89 — just above the SCRAP threshold. All were false positives.

| 4
| Petra started overriding decisions one by one. She had no structured way to explain why. Each override was logged but never aggregated or reviewed.

| 5
| On day two Petra raised a verbal complaint with her shift lead. The shift lead assumed it was a bad batch of steel and took no action. No escalation path to the ML team existed.

| 6
| After three days, Sven (ML Engineer) noticed an anomalous spike in the override rate while reviewing the weekly report. He began investigating manually.

| 7
| Sven identified the lighting change as the root cause by cross-referencing maintenance logs. He recalibrated the model. False positive rate returned to baseline.

| 8
| The 340 panels that Petra had passed via override were never systematically reviewed. Her justification notes read "visual check OK" — no structured reason code existed.
|===

=== Informal Terms Heard

[cols="3,7", options="header"]
|===
| Term heard | What the expert seems to mean

| "the blink"
| The red warning light flash at the HMI when a defect is detected

| "a false"
| A false positive — operator shorthand for an incorrect SCRAP decision

| "ghost scratch"
| A scratch the model detects that does not physically exist — often a lighting artefact

| "the override button"
| The confirmation action on Petra's HMI panel that logs a human correction

| "a bad batch"
| Shift lead assumption: product quality issue, not a model issue

| "the weekly"
| The weekly override rate report reviewed by the ML Engineer

| "lighting flip"
| The informal name for the halogen-to-LED transition event on Line 3

| "drift window"
| Informal concept: the undetected period between model drift onset and discovery
|===

=== Surprises and Unknowns — Red Hotspots

[cols="1,9", options="header"]
|===
| # | Surprise / unknown

| ⚠1
| Nobody owns the override log. It exists, but no one is responsible for reviewing it, aggregating patterns, or acting on what it contains.

| ⚠2
| The model confidence score is never shown to Petra on the HMI. She sees only the Decision (SCRAP / PASS), not the underlying confidence that produced it.

| ⚠3
| There is no formal escalation path from Quality Engineer to ML Engineer. Drift surfaces only if the ML Engineer happens to notice an anomaly in a weekly report.

| ⚠4
| Override reason codes do not exist. Petra's justification is free text or verbal. "Visual check OK" cannot drive retraining or satisfy EU AI Act audit requirements.

| ⚠5
| RCA reports are generated by the cloud LLM system but no one has confirmed they are read, actioned, or linked back to model updates.

| ⚠6
| Environmental context — lighting, temperature, shift — is not logged alongside inspection data. Model drift caused by environmental change is indistinguishable from model degradation.

| ⚠7
| The decision band thresholds (confidence → decision class) were set at initial model deployment. Nobody knows who owns them or when they were last reviewed.
|===
