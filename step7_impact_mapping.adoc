[.lead]
This document provides two things for Step 7 of the VQS DDD Workshop:

. A *blank template* for facilitating an Impact Mapping session (Section 1).
. A *fully worked example* for the VQS system connecting all deliverables to a measurable business goal (Section 2).

Duration: *45 minutes*. +
Goal: Connect every deliverable to measurable business value. Cut anything without a path to the goal. Outputs are features. Impacts are behaviour changes. Do not confuse them.

=== The Four Levels of an Impact Map

[cols="2,5,3", options="header"]
|===
| Level | Question | Example

| *Goal*
| What are we trying to achieve? Must be measurable and time-bound.
| "Reduce defect escape rate by 30% in 6 months"

| *Actors*
| Who needs to behave differently for the goal to be reached?
| Quality Engineer, ML Engineer, Plant Manager, Regulator

| *Impacts*
| How should the actor's behaviour change?
| "QE makes override decisions in under 30 seconds"

| *Deliverables*
| What do we build to enable the impact?
| "Real-time HMI alert with thumbnail and reason code selector"
|===

[WARNING]
====
The most common mistake in Impact Mapping is confusing *Impacts* (behaviour changes) with *Deliverables* (features). An Impact is something an actor does differently. A Deliverable is something the team ships. A dashboard is a deliverable. Seeing defect trends earlier and making better staffing decisions is the impact the dashboard enables.
====

=== The Cut List

Every deliverable that cannot be traced to an actor impact on the goal map belongs on the cut list. Put it there explicitly. This is the most valuable output of the session.

=== Cut List — Deliverables with No Impact Path

[cols="5,5", options="header"]
|===
| Deliverable | Why it was cut

| Automated retraining pipeline (R3-01, R3-02 deferred from MVP)
| No actor needs automated retraining to achieve the 30% escape rate reduction in 6 months. Manual-trigger retraining is sufficient. Moves to Release 3. Revisit at 3-month review.

| Plant Manager shift dashboard (R3-04 deferred from MVP)
| The AuditLog export API (D-REG-1) already enables the Plant Manager to get the data she needs in MVP. A dedicated dashboard adds UX value but does not directly improve escape rate. Moves to Release 3.

| Environmental context logging (R3-03 deferred)
| Sensor infrastructure for lighting and temperature is not available at MVP timeline. Required to improve DriftSignal quality — schedule for Release 3 when sensor rollout is confirmed.

| Synthetic data generation (R3-05 deferred)
| DataGap detection is sufficient for MVP. Automated SyntheticSample generation requires GenAI pipeline work estimated at 6 sprints. Defer until Release 3.

| Override rate trend visualisation (R2-05 deferred from MVP)
| ML Engineer can monitor override rates via raw AuditLog export in MVP. Visualisation is a Release 2 quality-of-life improvement. No actor's behaviour changes if it is delayed from MVP.

| Explainability dashboard for operators
| No actor requested this. The ConfidenceScore is available in the AuditEntry but exposing it on the HMI requires a UI investment not linked to any identified impact. Parked — raise if escape rate target is missed.
|===
